Thoughts

There is a lot of cool things to spend time on. But I never seem to have enough time. I guess its a good sign when you love what you do so much that running out of time in a day to do it is the only major annoyance that you run into on a regular basis.

But once you find a career that you love the next question is how far will you go? I am not just talking about how long you are a web developer or how long you intend to stay in the rat race. I’m asking you if you are ready to really stretch you mind to understand coding. I am a student in the BYU Information Systems major where I have seen the difference between people who try to understand what they are coding and the people who just move along getting the job done.

Those who did not try to “get it” ended up struggling with difficult concepts and implementations.

I only ask you to give it your best shot. Sure, you don’t have to understand everything that’s going on under the hood but try to understand the logic of it. Don’t just copy and paste code blindly. I have found it quite helpful to copy lines of code by hand because it engages me to see what is actually going on. That way, when I need to tweak a few things I know where to go to make the change.

Just remember, people who are talented in their career are there because they sacrificed some time to make sure they really “got it”.
